Labor Force Participation Rate by Disability Status and Age, 2023

This bar chart illustrates the labor force participation rates of individuals with and without disabilities, segmented by age group. The x-axis represents age ranges (16-19, 20-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45-54, 55-64), while the y-axis represents labor force participation rates as a percentage. Each age group contains two bars: one for people with disabilities and one for people without disabilities.

  • Young Workers (16-19, 20-24):
    • Participation is lowest in the 16-19 age group, with both disabled and non-disabled individuals having the lowest workforce engagement.
    • The rate increases in the 20-24 age range, though a significant gap remains between the two groups.
  • Prime Working Years (25-34, 35-44, 45-54):
    • Non-disabled individuals have high participation rates across these years, peaking between 35-44.
    • Disabled individuals also experience their highest participation in this range but at substantially lower rates than their non-disabled counterparts.
  • Older Workers (55-64):
    • Participation rates decline for both groups as they near retirement age.
    • The gap between disabled and non-disabled workers remains evident, though both groups show a downward trend.

This chart highlights the persistent labor force participation gap between disabled and non-disabled individuals across all age groups. Younger and older disabled individuals have particularly low participation rates, while the disparity is most pronounced in prime working years (25-54).
