When I Hear “Accessibility”

When I hear accessiblity I hear making places, sites, and products user friendly for every user in the world to the best we can. I personally find accessiblity interesting to learn because, I've been learning a lot more of it at my job and here in this class. I think that accessbility is very essential for all people, we need to make sure our design is inclusive for all users. We should give them the best experience using our sites otherwise the sites may be redundant and frustrating for our users if we don't think about their needs.

Automated Tools are NOT Enough

Automated accessibility tools like WAVE are great for catching quick issues. Wave can also help us fix the fast errors but, doesn't go into the deeper errors. However, it can help us with correcting color contrast.

Accessibility Standards I Use

The web standards I know are the WCAG standards for web compliance have been devolped by the W3C to help us use these guidelines to make sites user friendly for all users. The 508 act requires us to be accomadating for those with disabilities. With the WCAG guidelines there's a minimum of requirements that must be met such as level standards.

Why Inclusion During Design/Development Matters

Including people of all backgrounds and abilities in testing and design means the product will work for everyone especially for users with disabilities. When sites aren't user friendly it can frustrate users especially if they don't have visual or physical aids to help.

Conformance status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. Lauren Website is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.
