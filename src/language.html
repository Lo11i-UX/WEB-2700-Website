Plain Language: What is it? And how do I do it?

Plain language means writing in a way that your audience can easily understand. It focuses on clarity, simplicity, and structure. Whether it's a webpage, email, or document, plain language helps your message come through clearly, especially for people who may be unfamiliar with technical terms or who use screen readers or translation tools.

What Are the Key Guidelines?

Know your audience:

Tailor your message to your readersâ€™ needs, expectations, and background. Your audience wants to grasp the message quickly

Organize your content:

Start with the most important points. Use headings and subheadings. Use bullet points and numbered lists. Reduces the reader's cognitive load.

Use Plain and Simple Language:

Avoid technical jargon, complex words, and convoluted sentences. Keep your message clear and accessible.

Use active voice:

Active voice makes your writing direct and energetic. While passive voice may appear occasionally, active voice is preferred.

Stay consistent:

Consistency in style and terminology enhances clarity. Use uniform language throughout your document.

Summary

Plain language guidelines are essential tools for clear communication. They help you produce transparent and effective content. Although sticking to these principles is ideal, slight deviations may occur as you balance clarity with the nuances of language.

Verbs

Original Sentence Rewritten Reasoning
The report was completed. The team completed the report. Passive to active voice.
Implementation of new regulations will have been carried out by the compliance team by the end of the year The compliance team will implement the new regulations by the end of the year. Complex to simple verb phrase.
The teacher gave instructions, was explaining the project, and then assigns the homework. The teacher gave instructions, explained the project, and then assigned homework. Inconsistent to consistent tense.

Nouns

Original Sentence Rewritten Reasoning
The implementation of the new policy resulted in confusion among employees. The new policy confused employees. Passive/abstract to active/direct.
The CEO announced the acquisition of XYZ Corp during the AGM. Our CEO announced we acquired XYZ Corp during the AGM. More conversational and direct subject-verb structure.
Delivery of the presentation is scheduled for tomorrow at the HQ. Our presentation is tomorrow at HQ. Simplified and natural phrasing.

Other Word Issues

Original Sentence Rewritten Reasoning
The amelioration of the project's outcomes was achieved through meticulous data analysis to foster enhancement. Careful data analysis improved the project's outcome. Wordy and abstract to concise and clear.
The concert will take place on Saturday, weather permitting, and is anticipated to be attended by approximately 200 individuals. The concert is scheduled for Saturday and about 200 people are expected to attend. Replaces formal or vague phrasing with more direct language.
The compliance team conducted the audit to ensure adherence to the ISO standards, but many team members were unfamiliar with these requirements. The compliance team audited for ISO standards, but many members were unfamiliar with them. Simplified structure and reduced redundancy.
The architect utilized a cantilevered design to optimize spatial integrity, unbeknownst to the client. The architect used a cantilevered design to make the space more open, without the client knowing. Uses clearer, more conversational language in place of overly technical or archaic words.

Sentences

Original Sentence Rewritten Reasoning
Despite the widespread recognition of the importance of sustainability in corporate practices, there are numerous organizations that continue to prioritize short-term profits, which ultimately detracts from the long-term viability of both the companies and the communities in which they operate. Many organizations know sustainability is important but still prioritize short-term profits, harming their long-term success and the communities they serve. Breaks up a long, complex sentence into a clearer and more direct structure.
It was determined by the committee after several lengthy discussions and considerations of various factors, including budget constraints and projected timelines, that the proposed renovation of the community center should be postponed until further notice. After budget and timeline discussions, the committee postponed the renovation until further notice. Passive to active voice with concise rephrasing.
Provided that the weather remains favorable and there are no unexpected delays during the installation process, the solar panels will be operational, thereby contributing to the facility's energy efficiency, by the end of the quarter. If weather holds and there are no delays, the solar panels will be operational by quarter's end, boosting energy efficiency. Simplifies structure and uses everyday language.

Paragraphs

Original Paragraph Rewritten Reasoning
The environmental impacts of urbanization have been significant, particularly concerning the increase in air pollution levels and the reduction in green spaces due to the expansion of infrastructure. Cities have been experiencing a rise in air pollutants resulting from increased vehicular emissions and industrial activities. This has been exacerbated by the reduction in natural environments, which has decreased the capacity for city landscapes to filter out pollution naturally. Noise pollution has also become predominant, with the increasing population density leading to a surge in transportation noise and general urban activity. These environmental challenges have prompted cities to adopt various measures aimed at mitigating their impacts, such as the implementation of green roofs and the creation of urban parks. Moreover, public transportation systems have been expanded and improved to reduce reliance on personal vehicles. Efforts to promote sustainable practices are continuing, as are initiatives to enhance urban resilience in the face of environmental challenges. Urbanization has increased air and noise pollution while reducing green spaces. Rising vehicle use and industrial activity contribute to poor air quality, and fewer natural areas mean less pollution is filtered naturally. Cities are responding by adding green roofs, urban parks, and expanding public transportation to promote sustainability and reduce environmental impact. Condenses the paragraph, removes redundancy, and uses more direct, reader-friendly language.

Verbs Demystified

Learn about verb usage by expanding the sections below.

What is Active Voice?

  • Definition: In an active sentence, the subject performs the action.
  • Example: "The cat chased the mouse." (Here, "the cat" is doing the chasing.)

What is Passive Voice?

  • Definition: Passive sentences often use forms of "to be" plus a past participle.
  • Example: "The mouse was chased by the cat." (Here, "the mouse" receives the action.)

How to Switch from Passive to Active:

  • Step 1: Identify the subject (the doer).
    Passive: "The mouse was chased by the cat."
  • Step 2: Move the doer to the beginning.
    Active: "The cat chased the mouse."
  • Tip: If the doer is missing, add it.
    Passive: "The report was completed."
    Active: "The team completed the report."

Why Use Active Voice?

  • Clearer and more direct senten~ces
  • More engaging and powerful writing
  • Better for instructions, persuasion, and clear communication
